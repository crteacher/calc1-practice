The Derivative at a Point

Calculus I  ·  §2.2  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Definition (h-form — use this for calculations) $$f'(c) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(c+h) - f(c)}{h}$$ Expand $f(c+h)$, subtract $f(c)$, cancel the $h$ in the denominator, then evaluate.
Tangent Line at x = c $$y = f'(c)(x - c) + f(c)$$ Slope equals $f'(c)$, passing through $(c,\, f(c))$. If $f'(c) > 0$: increasing.   If $f'(c) < 0$: decreasing.
