Implicit Differentiation

Calculus I  ·  §3.1  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Implicit Differentiation Differentiate both sides w.r.t. $x$. Every $y$-term picks up $\frac{dy}{dx}$ (chain rule). Collect and solve. $$\frac{d}{dx}[x^2+y^2] = \frac{d}{dx}[r^2] \;\Rightarrow\; 2x+2y\frac{dy}{dx}=0 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{dy}{dx}=-\frac{x}{y}$$
Product Rule on Mixed Terms $$\frac{d}{dx}[xy] = y + x\frac{dy}{dx} \qquad \frac{d}{dx}[x^2y] = 2xy + x^2\frac{dy}{dx}$$ To find slope at a point: get $\frac{dy}{dx}$ as a formula in $x$ and $y$, then substitute.
Practice Problems

Symbolic: write like -x/y, -3x/(2y), -(2x+y)/(x+2y). Numeric (slope at a point): integer or fraction like -3/4. Press Check or hit Enter.
