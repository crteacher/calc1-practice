Core Ideas
FTC Part 1 — Accumulation Function If $F(x)=\displaystyle\int_a^x f(t)\,dt$, then $F'(x)=f(x)$.
$F$ is increasing where $f>0$, decreasing where $f<0$. To find $F(c)$: use FTC Part 2 to evaluate $\int_a^c f(t)\,dt$.
Chain Rule Version $$\frac{d}{dx}\int_a^{g(x)} f(t)\,dt = f(g(x))\cdot g'(x)$$ Identify $g(x)$, evaluate $f$ at $g(x)$, multiply by $g'(x)$.
