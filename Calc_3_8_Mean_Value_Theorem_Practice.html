Mean Value Theorem

Calculus I  ·  §3.8  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Mean Value Theorem If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and differentiable on $(a,b)$, then $\exists\; c\in(a,b)$ such that $$f'(c)=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}$$ Instantaneous rate of change $=$ average rate of change at some interior point.
Finding c  |  Rolle's Theorem Compute $m=\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}$, then solve $f'(x)=m$ and keep solutions in $(a,b)$. Rolle's Theorem: special case where $f(a)=f(b)$, so $m=0$ and $f'(c)=0$.
