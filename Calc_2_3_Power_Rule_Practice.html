The Power Rule

Calculus I  ·  §2.3  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
The Power Rule & Friends $$\frac{d}{dx}\left[x^n\right] = nx^{n-1} \qquad \frac{d}{dx}\left[cf(x)\right] = cf'(x) \qquad \frac{d}{dx}\left[f \pm g\right] = f' \pm g'$$ Apply term by term. Constants differentiate to $0$. Rewrite roots as fractional exponents before differentiating.
Using f' Tangent line at $x=c$: $y = f'(c)(x-c)+f(c)$.   $f'(x)=0$ gives horizontal tangents (critical points).
