Differentiability

Calculus I  ·  §2.2.6  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Differentiability $f$ is differentiable at $c$ if $f'(c)$ exists. Three failure modes: discontinuity, corner (left and right slopes differ), vertical tangent (slope is infinite). For piecewise functions: check both continuity AND equal slopes at the join point.
Key Theorem Differentiable $\Rightarrow$ Continuous  (always).   Continuous $\Rightarrow$ Differentiable  (NOT always — $|x|$ at 0 is the classic counterexample).
Proof: $\lim_{x\to c}[f(x)-f(c)] = \lim_{x\to c}\frac{f(x)-f(c)}{x-c}\cdot(x-c) = f'(c)\cdot 0 = 0$.
