Riemann Sums

Calculus I  ·  §4.2  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Riemann Sum Setup Partition $[a,b]$ into $n$ subintervals of width $\Delta x = \dfrac{b-a}{n}$. $$L_n = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} f(x_i)\,\Delta x \qquad R_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n} f(x_i)\,\Delta x$$ Left sum uses left endpoints; right sum uses right endpoints; midpoint uses interval centers.
Over / Underestimates  |  Sigma Formulas Increasing $f$: left sum underestimates, right sum overestimates. Decreasing $f$: opposite.    $\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}i = \tfrac{n(n+1)}{2}$     $\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^{n}i^2 = \tfrac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$
