Antiderivatives & Initial Value Problems

Calculus I  ·  §4.5  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Antiderivative Rules Power rule: $\displaystyle\int x^n\,dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+C$  $(n\neq -1)$     Constant: $\displaystyle\int k\,dx = kx+C$
Integrate term by term; pull constants outside. Always verify by differentiating.
Initial Value Problems Given $F'(x)=f(x)$ and $F(a)=b$: antidifferentiate to get $F(x)=\cdots+C$, then substitute $F(a)=b$ to solve for $C$.   Shortcut: if $F(0)=d$, all non-constant terms vanish and $C=d$ directly.
