Displacement, Velocity & Acceleration

Calculus I  ·  §4.6  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Position · Velocity · Acceleration $v(t)=s'(t)$    $a(t)=v'(t)=s''(t)$
Going back up: $v(t)=\int a(t)\,dt+C$ (use $v(t_0)$ to find $C$);   $s(t)=\int v(t)\,dt+C$ (use $s(t_0)$).
Displacement $$\Delta s = \int_a^b v(t)\,dt = s(b)-s(a)$$ Displacement is signed (can be negative). Total distance $= \int_a^b |v(t)|\,dt$ (always $\geq 0$).
