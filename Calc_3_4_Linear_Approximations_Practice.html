Linear Approximations

Calculus I  ·  §3.4  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Linear Approximation Formula $$L(x) = f(a) + f'(a)(x-a)$$ $$\frac{d}{dx}[x^2]\Big|_{x=3}=6 \;\Rightarrow\; L(x)=9+6(x-3)=6x-9 \;\Rightarrow\; 3.1^2\approx L(3.1)=9.6$$
Standard Approximations $\frac{d}{dx}[e^x]\big|_{x=0}=1$: $L(x)=x+1$  —  $e^{0.2}\approx 1.2$
$\frac{d}{dx}[\ln x]\big|_{x=1}=1$: $L(x)=x-1$  —  $\ln(1.3)\approx 0.3$
$\frac{d}{dx}[\sqrt{x}]\big|_{x=4}=\tfrac{1}{4}$: $L(x)=2+\tfrac{x-4}{4}$  —  $\sqrt{4.4}\approx 2.1$
