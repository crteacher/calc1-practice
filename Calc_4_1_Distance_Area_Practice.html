Measuring Distance Traveled / Area Under a Curve

Calculus I  ·  §4.1  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
Area = Accumulated Change $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$ = area under $f$ from $a$ to $b$. If $f$ is a rate of change (e.g.\ speed), the integral gives total change (distance). For simple shapes use geometry; otherwise approximate with Riemann sums.
Riemann Sums from a Table Left sum: use $f$ at the left endpoint of each subinterval.   Right sum: use $f$ at the right endpoint. Width of each rectangle = gap between consecutive $x$-values (can vary). Total $\approx \sum f(x_i)\cdot\Delta x_i$
Practice Problems

All answers are integers. Table problems: multiply f-value × width for each subinterval and sum. Equal-width problems: compute Δx = (b−a)/n first. Velocity problem: enter total miles. Press Check or hit Enter.
