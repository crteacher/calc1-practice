Limits

Calculus I  ·  §1.1  ·  Instructor: Christopher Halverson  ·  Fall 2026

Core Ideas
The Limit $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to a} f(x)=L$   means $f(x)$ approaches $L$ as $x\to a$ (without $x$ ever equaling $a$). $f(a)$ may be undefined — the limit only depends on values near $a$.
Existence & DNE The limit exists iff the function approaches the same value from both the left and the right. If the two sides approach different values, the limit does not exist (DNE).
For polynomials: substitute directly.  For $\tfrac{0}{0}$: factor and cancel.
